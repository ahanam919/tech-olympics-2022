If the name “Not to Do” is perplexing, just look at this page as “Top 10 Ways to Get Into a Sticky Situation (which is sometimes illegal)”.

Let’s get right in!

Consuming Medicines

  • ALWAYS read the label on the bottles you’re using, or the sharps that you need to administer.
  • If it's a sharp, and you haven’t used one before, make sure you talk with a doctor about inserting it in correctly.
  • TAKE WARNINGS SERIOUSLY! Overdose can result in lengthy and painful hospital visits, if you’re lucky. Otherwise you could die.

Disposal

  • General Disposal 
  • Do NOT crush up pills or tablets, throw them whole.
  • First rule of thumb: Even if it is an OTC, check to see if it’s for home disposal.
  • Important: DO NOT FLUSH MEDICATION DOWN THE TOILET. (unless otherwise instructed by a doctor/pharmacist)
  • Pharmaceutical Disposal 
  • Entering and just dumping your bag of medicine on the nearest cashier’s counter
  • This is partly a joke, but please do not come to the counter with a lump of cream or a pile of pills clinging for dear life in your cupped palms.
